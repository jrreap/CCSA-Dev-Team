Hagley Museum and Library

History

Hagley Powder Mill was built by Éleuthère Irénée du Pont de Nemours in 1803 on the banks of the brandywine river. He chose to build the factory here because of the power the water provided, the abundance of timber and willow in the area that could be used to make quality charcoal as an ingredient in making good cahrcoal, and its proximity to the delaware river to ship in the other chemicals. The entire DuPont Company started from this powder mill. In 1912 the mill itself closed and individaul peices of the land were sold off. In 1952 on the 150th Anniversery of the Dupont company plans for a Hagley Museum and Library were revealed.
